Developers' Society

Collaborate. Innovate. Propagate.

Collaborate. We foster an inclusive & collaborative community of all backgrounds. / Whether experienced or beginning, into code or design, we gather passionate individuals that continuously learn from one another and grow deep bonds through various community activities and events.

Innovate.Believing in programming and code as essential skills no matter what your field of expertise lies in, we host workshops, outreaches, game jams, hackathons, and teach people of all skill levels and ages how to program.

We inspire innovation of all kinds, whether multimedia, film, writing, or game development -- and integrate it into technology. Supporting developers within the organization, we help mixed talents create concrete products; a hub of innovation for game development, startups, projects, and more.

Propagate. It's not enough to create. Importantly, we share these creations and ensure that they are reached out to the masses. Disrupting and defying today's industries and issues with creations from the youth, we share and support developers and creatives -- ensuring that they can continue innovating with the right tools and audience. Onwards, we propagate technology and share its significance to each and every individual.

What do I do as a core member?

As a core member, you work closely in ensuring the success of Developers' Society's events. Information about future and ongoing projects are located within the primer but these are just a fragment of our projects.

  1. Event development: Act as event staff based on your personal skills (whether marketing, logistics, finance, etc.) & assist in plotting out various projects, ensuring their success in achieving our Vision-Mission.
  2. Event formation: Pioneer new programs and events or overhaul former ones. Spearhead the development of these projects — whether a fundraiser, convention, workshop, or more.
  3. Curriculum development: Core members that are also proficient in code, multimedia, or design (3D modeling, worldbuilding, writing, etc.) or even all three are also part of the Internal Committee, the body responsible for the development of Developers' Society's technology education curriculum and projects. They design the teaching program for workshops and outreaches based on their expertise and assist in the development of in-organization projects and programs. Promoting tech education, teaching others, and learning new things from our collaborative community as well.
  4. Marketing & communications: Assisting in the promotion and sharing of organization projects in order to have a broader reach and impact — core members, hailing from different backgrounds, take part in ensuring that events are diverse while fostering a supportive and inclusive environment.
  5. In-organization projects: Closely connected to all of the organization's activities, core members help facilitate or even partake in projects being built and developed within the organization and its teams. Home to different projects, big and small, from games, applications, websites, creative writing, blogs, and even startups — core members ensure the continuous development of such projects, and even partake in these themselves in the org's goal of creating concrete innovations.
  6. Ambassador program: Core members may also represent their schools in spreading Developers' Society's reach towards them, attracting more members to the organization and events as they build a stronger community engrossed in technology.
  7. Enter our family! ♥ Developers' Society's core are not just staff members that want to work and just happen to be grouped together. We're all passionate individuals that love technology in different ways. We gather together for this common love and bond with one another. The most important part about Developers' Society is the community we create, and the creations that come from it.

In the application, core members have an option to apply for officer positions. Not all core members are officers, but they may be elevated towards the position if they express interest and are deemed fit to perform the task. Otherwise, they're just general core members that perform non-specialized tasks.
